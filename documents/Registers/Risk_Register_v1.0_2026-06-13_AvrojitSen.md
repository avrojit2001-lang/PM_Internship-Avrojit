# **Risk Register — Expense Tracker App**

# **Introduction**

The purpose of this Risk Register is to systematically identify, assess, and manage potential risks that may impact the successful delivery of the Expense Tracker App project. By maintaining this central repository of threats and opportunities, the project team can implement proactive measures to safeguard project timelines, quality, and budget.

Effective risk management is a continuous process. These risks will be monitored and updated throughout the project lifecycle to reflect the evolving nature of the development environment and stakeholder requirements.

# **Notes on Risk Management**

## **Risk Scoring Calculation**

The Risk Score is calculated by multiplying **Probability** (Likelihood) by **Impact** (Severity). Both factors are rated on a scale of 1 to 3 (Low \= 1, Medium \= 2, High \= 3), resulting in a maximum possible score of 9\. This quantitative approach helps prioritize risks that require immediate attention.

## **Response Strategies**

* **Avoid:** Changing the project plan to eliminate the threat entirely (e.g., clarifying requirements to prevent scope creep).  
* **Mitigate:** Taking action to reduce the probability or the impact of a risk (e.g., rigorous testing for critical features).  
* **Transfer:** Shifting the impact of a threat to a third party (e.g., hiring a temporary contractor or purchasing insurance).  
* **Accept:** Acknowledging the risk and not taking any action unless the risk occurs (typically used for low-probability risks where the cost of mitigation exceeds the impact).

## **Ownership and Accountability**

Assigning a clear **Risk Owner** for each identified risk is essential for accountability. The owner is responsible for monitoring their assigned risk, implementing the chosen response strategy, and reporting status updates to the project leadership.

# **Conclusion**

This Risk Register serves as a cornerstone for proactive project management within the Expense Tracker App development. By identifying challenges early, we transition from reactive crisis management to strategic planning. Continuous monitoring and frequent updates to this document are essential, as new risks may emerge and existing ones may change in severity as the project evolves.
